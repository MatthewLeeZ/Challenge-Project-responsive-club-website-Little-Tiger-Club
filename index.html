Practices

"True victory is victory over oneself." – Morihei Ueshiba

At Little Tiger Club, our disciplines are not just martial arts but paths of inner transformation. Through three complementary practices – Kung Fu, Taichi, and Qigong – we invite you to explore the power of discipline, the fluidity of movement, and the harmony of vital energy. Discover below the unique aspects of each path, guiding you to push your limits and grow, with body and mind in perfect synergy.

Kung-Fu

"I fear not the man who has practiced 10,000 kicks once, but I fear the man who has practiced one kick 10,000 times." – Bruce Lee

Kung Fu is the pure expression of controlled power. Every strike, every stance, is the result of rigorous training, where speed, strength, and precision reach their peak. Here, endurance forges the body like steel, and repetition transforms effort into instinct. Through practice, muscles grow stronger, reflexes sharpen, and power becomes second nature, ready to be unleashed at any moment.

Awakening emerges in an instant.”



Shaolin Monk Zhan Ju

Tai-Chi

"In movement, seek tranquility; in tranquility, be ready for movement." – Chen Fake

Taichi is more than a sequence of slow, fluid movements. It is a refined martial art that combines breath control, precise gestures, and deep body awareness.

Behind its gentleness lies a demanding discipline that strengthens muscles without brutality. Through grounded postures and controlled motions, practitioners develop balance and stability, essential for both physical and mental challenges.

Beyond strength, Taichi cultivates an inner power that channels energy efficiently. Rather than opposing force, it absorbs, redirects, and transforms it, fostering adaptability and resilience.

By practicing with calm determination, the body gains flexibility and endurance, while the mind learns to refocus. More than an art of movement, Taichi is a path to balance, where each step leads to lasting harmony.

Qi-Gong

"He who conquers others is strong. He who conquers himself is powerful." – Lao-Tzu

Qigong is the ancient art of circulating vital energy, a bridge between heaven and earth, between man and the infinite.

Rooted in shamanic, Taoist, and Buddhist traditions, it unites breath, movement, and intention to purify the body, calm the mind, and awaken consciousness.

Through fluid gestures and grounded postures, the practitioner absorbs the energy of the earth and opens to the vastness of the sky.

By focusing on the breath, they nourish their Qi, the vital force flowing through the meridians, revitalizing the body and mind.

At the heart of this practice lies the Dantian, the reservoir of deep energy where true power is cultivated.

More than an exercise, Qigong is a moving meditation, a path to inner balance and harmony.

For centuries, this mystical art has guided seekers toward strength, serenity, and a profound connection with the universe.

Instructor

"A master is a student who has never stopped learning."

Master Sifu grew up in these mountains, raised by Golden Teacher, flexible and resilient like bamboo in a storm. From childhood, he carried heavy shells on his back, never knowing why.

"One day, you will understand," his master would say with a laugh. He ran barefoot on rocky trails, practiced katas under the moon, and tried to catch the wind with his hands.

But above all, he learned to laugh. For while his master was wise, he was also a trickster. Sometimes, he would give him impossible riddles.

"Can one catch their shadow with their hands?"

Today, Master Sifu has become a master himself. He teaches Kung Fu, Taichi, and Qigong at a most unusual school, an academy of anthropomorphic cats. These agile and mischievous creatures are his students, and he trains them to master their breath, their balance, their mind, and the art of combat.

Yet, deep down, he remains a disciple. He knows he still has much to learn from his master, who sometimes appears in his dreams to ask him strange yet profound questions.

"When the moon reflects in the water, who is looking at whom?" his master once asked. To this day, Master Sifu has not found the answer.

Despite his age and wisdom, Master Sifu has one weakness, women. He can face a storm, outmaneuver an opponent with a flick of his wrist, but when met with a charming gaze, he loses all composure.

Golden Teacher still teases him about it.

Lost in mist-shrouded mountains, where rivers whisper to the rocks and pines dance with the wind, lives a master known as Golden Teacher.

No one knows his true age. Some whisper that he was already there before the birth of emperors, while others swear he taught the first dragons how to ride the clouds. What is certain is that his gaze pierces the illusion of the world, and his laughter echoes like cascading waterfalls.

Golden Teacher is a master of Taichi and Qigong, but above all, a master of Wu Wei—the art of harmonizing with the natural flow of the universe. His Kung Fu, honed over time, is a perfect balance between the fluidity of water and the power of thunder. He teaches that the fist must be as light as a feather yet as sharp as a blade, that strength does not reside in muscles but in the understanding of emptiness.

The few disciples who have received his teachings say that he could strike without touching, evade without moving, and fight without violence. His Kung Fu is a mystery—a dance with the invisible, a technique that defies logic as much as it elevates it.

But that is not his only talent. He heals through energy, with a mere touch or a word that resonates deep within the soul. He seems to see beyond the present, sensing events before they unfold, reading the threads of destiny as others would read an open book. His disciples whisper that he not only sees the future but also the thoughts and hearts of men, perceiving their deepest fears and most hidden desires in a single glance.

And yet, despite his unfathomable wisdom, Golden Teacher is an incorrigible trickster. He vanishes in the middle of a fight, reappears behind you biting into a fruit, and teaches the art of combat...

Location

"Enter with respect, leave with humility."

Nestled in the misty mountains, between heaven and earth, lies the Hermitage of Spirit and Time, a sanctuary where one comes to refine both body and mind.

On one side, a traditional building, crafted from ancient wood and dark tiles, stands as a testament to the ancestors’ respect for harmony between man and nature. Its carved doors open onto a tranquil courtyard, where the echoes of training or the whispers of the wind through the leaves can sometimes be heard.

On the other, a river winds through the forest, caressing a massive rock engraved with golden calligraphy. Here, the water never rushes; it flows with the patience of time, carrying the secrets of past generations. Those who sit upon this stone feel the weight of silence and the lingering wisdom of Golden Teacher’s teachings.

This place is not merely a dojo, but a retreat. One comes here to train, to meditate, and sometimes to sleep under the gentle protection of the mountains. Far from the turmoil of the world, every visitor learns that the true battle is within, and that discipline must walk hand in hand with humility.

On the sacred mountain,

Like thoughts in the mind of the sage,

Leaving the mountain unchanged.

In its eternal presence,

It simply remains.
